Utility Assistance

Help paying electric, gas, and water bills in San Bernardino County

LIHEAP – Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program

Federal program providing energy bill assistance and crisis intervention for eligible low-income San Bernardino County residents. Covers electric, gas, and cooling costs.

📞 909-891-3000 🌐 San Bernardino County Human Services
Eligibility: Income at or below 150% of federal poverty level · Contact: 909-891-3000
Energy

CARE / FERA – California Alternate Rate for Energy

California utility discount programs offering 20-30% off monthly electric and gas bills for qualifying low-income San Bernardino County households.

📞 1-800-627-1111 🌐 cpuc.ca.gov/low-income
Electric

211 – 211 San Bernardino County Utility Crisis Referral

Call 211 anytime for immediate referrals to emergency utility assistance programs in San Bernardino County, including after-hours crisis resources.

📞 Dial 211 🌐 www.211sb.org
Referral

Housing & Rental Assistance

Rent help, eviction prevention, and housing stability programs

San Bernardino County Housing Authority – Emergency Rental Assistance

Emergency rental and mortgage assistance for San Bernardino County residents facing eviction or foreclosure due to financial hardship.

📞 909-890-9533 🌐 www.hacsb.com
Rental Aid

Section 8 Housing Choice Voucher Program

Federally-funded rental assistance vouchers for income-qualifying households in San Bernardino County. Apply through your local housing authority when waitlists open.

📞 909-890-9533 🌐 www.hacsb.com
Section 8

California Homeowner Assistance Fund (HAF)

Mortgage assistance for homeowners in San Bernardino County facing financial hardship. Covers mortgage payments, property taxes, insurance, and HOA fees.

📞 1-888-240-0555 🌐 camortgagerelief.org

Food Assistance

CalFresh, food banks, WIC, and free meal programs

CalFresh – California Food Assistance (SNAP)

Apply for monthly food benefits through BenefitsCal. CalFresh helps San Bernardino County residents buy groceries with an EBT card. Average benefit: $200+/month per person.

📞 1-877-847-3663 🌐 benefitscal.com
Apply online at BenefitsCal.com or visit San Bernardino County Human Services in San Bernardino · Processing: 30 days (3 days expedited)
CalFresh

Feeding America Inland Empire

Local food bank serving San Bernardino County residents. Find the nearest food pantry, mobile distribution, or partner agency near you.

📞 909-514-1343 🌐 www.feedingamericaie.org
Food Bank

WIC – Women, Infants & Children Program

Monthly food benefits, nutrition counseling, and breastfeeding support for pregnant women, new mothers, and children under age 5 in San Bernardino County.

📞 1-800-843-2637 🌐 cdph.ca.gov/wic
WIC

Healthcare & Medi-Cal

Free and low-cost healthcare, Medi-Cal enrollment, and clinics

Medi-Cal – California Medicaid Health Coverage

Free or low-cost health insurance for qualifying San Bernardino County residents including families, pregnant women, seniors, and people with disabilities.

📞 1-800-541-5555 🌐 benefitscal.com
Medi-Cal

Covered California – Health Insurance Marketplace

Shop for low-cost health insurance plans during open enrollment. San Bernardino County residents may qualify for significant subsidies to reduce monthly premiums.

📞 1-800-300-1506 🌐 coveredca.com
Insurance

Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs)

Community health centers in San Bernardino County offer primary care, dental, and mental health services on a sliding-fee scale regardless of insurance status.

📞 1-800-548-4433 🌐 findahealthcenter.hrsa.gov
Health Clinic

America's Job Center of California – Workforce Development

Job placement, resume assistance, career training, and employer connections through the America's Job Center serving San Bernardino County.

Programs for adults 60+ in San Bernardino County

Free Medicare counseling and health insurance guidance for San Bernardino County seniors. Helps navigate Medicare Part A/B/D, Medicare Advantage, and supplements.

California Department of Aging – San Bernardino County Senior Services

Connects San Bernardino County seniors to home-delivered meals (Meals on Wheels), transportation, in-home care, caregiver support, and community programs through the Area Agency on Aging.
