Do I Qualify?

Legal aid services are generally available to individuals and families earning below 125–200% of the Federal Poverty Level. Some programs accept cases based on the nature of the legal issue regardless of income (e.g., domestic violence, veterans' legal issues). Contact your local legal aid office for a free eligibility screening.

Low income individualsDomestic violence survivorsVeteransPeople with disabilitiesSeniorsImmigrants
Step-by-Step

How Do I Apply?

  • ✓
    Contact your local legal aid office. Find the legal aid organization serving your county on our county pages. Call their intake line to describe your legal issue and schedule a screening.
  • ✓
    Complete the intake process. An intake specialist will ask about your legal issue, income, and household size to determine eligibility. Have recent pay stubs or benefits letters ready.
  • ✓
    Get assigned an attorney. If you qualify, you'll be matched with a staff attorney or pro bono volunteer. They'll handle your case from consultation through resolution.
  • ✓
    Attend your hearings and appointments. Your attorney will prepare you for any court dates, hearings, or negotiations. Follow their guidance on deadlines and document requests.
  • ✓
    Know your self-help options. Even if you don't qualify for full representation, many legal aid offices offer self-help clinics, document preparation, and legal information hotlines.
