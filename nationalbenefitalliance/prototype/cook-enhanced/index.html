Utility Assistance

Help paying electric, gas, and water bills in Cook County

LIHEAP — Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program

Federal program providing energy bill assistance and crisis intervention for eligible low-income Cook County residents. Covers electric, gas, propane, and cooling costs. Administered locally by CEDA (Community & Economic Development Assoc.).

📞 1-833-711-0374 🌐 helpillinoisfamilies.com
Application Period 🗓️ Seasonal
Oct 1, 2025 – Aug 15, 2026 (or until funds exhausted)
Priority: Oct 1 for seniors 60+, disabled, families w/ children ≤5, and disconnected households. All others: Nov 1.
Eligibility Household income ≤ 60% of State Median Income (SMI)
1 person: $2,627/mo · 2 people: $3,436/mo
4 people: $5,055/mo · 6 people: $6,673/mo
Documents Needed
  • Proof of income (last 30 days) — pay stubs, SSA/VA benefit letters
  • Most recent utility bill (issued within 30 days)
  • Social Security numbers for all household members (new applicants)
  • Photo ID for head of household
How to Apply
🌐 Online Portal 📞 1-833-711-0374 🏢 Local CEDA Office
Energy

ComEd CARE — Customer Assistance & Rate Education

ComEd's assistance program offering reduced rates, bill payment plans, and budget billing for qualifying Cook County residents. Separate from LIHEAP — you can use both programs.

📞 1-800-334-7661 (ComEd) 🌐 ComEd Financial Assistance
Application Period 🔄 Rolling
Apply anytime. No seasonal deadline.
Eligibility Must be a ComEd residential customer. Income ≤ 200% FPL or enrolled in eligible assistance program (LIHEAP, Medicaid, SNAP).
Documents Needed
  • ComEd account number
  • Proof of income or enrollment in qualifying program
How to Apply
📞 1-800-334-7661 🌐 ComEd.com
Electric

211 Illinois — Utility Crisis Referral

Dial 211 anytime for immediate referrals to emergency utility assistance in Cook County, including after-hours crisis resources, LIHEAP enrollment, and utility company hardship programs.

📞 Dial 211 🌐 211.org
No application needed ✅ Available 24/7 — Free, confidential. Connects you to 1,700+ local programs across Cook County. No documents required.

Housing & Rental Assistance

Rent help, eviction prevention, and housing stability programs

Court-Based Rental Assistance Program (CBRAP) via IHDA

Emergency rental assistance for Cook County residents facing eviction. Provides up to $25,000 covering 15 months of past-due rent plus 3 months of future rent. Administered by the Illinois Housing Development Authority.

📞 1-866-454-3571 🌐 illinoishousinghelp.org/cbrap
Application Period ✅ Currently Open
Available to tenants and landlords with pending eviction court cases. Apply through your eviction court case.
Eligibility Must have an active eviction case in Cook County court. Income requirements vary. Covers up to $25,000 in rental payments.
Documents Needed
  • Proof of income (pay stubs, benefit letters)
  • Lease or rental agreement
  • Proof of financial hardship
  • Eviction court case number
How to Apply
🌐 Online at IHDA 📞 1-866-454-3571 ⚖️ Through eviction court
Rental Aid

Section 8 Housing Choice Voucher — HACC

Federally-funded rental subsidy vouchers administered by the Housing Authority of Cook County (HACC). Covers the difference between 30% of your income and fair market rent. Long waitlist — sign up for notifications.

📞 (312) 663-5447 🌐 thehacc.org
Application Period 🔒 Waitlist Closed
HCV waitlist last opened Oct 19–Nov 2, 2020. No announced reopening date. Project-based voucher waitlists open periodically — check thehacc.org.
Eligibility Household income ≤ 50% of Area Median Income (AMI)
1 person: ~$36,200/yr · 4 people: ~$51,700/yr
Exact limits set by HUD annually for Cook County.
How to Get Notified Sign up for email notifications at thehacc.org to be alerted when waitlists reopen. HACC office: 10 S. La Salle St., Suite 2200, Chicago, IL 60603 (by appointment only).
Section 8

Salvation Army — Emergency Housing & Shelter

Emergency shelter, transitional housing, and move-in assistance for Cook County residents facing homelessness. Includes security deposit assistance and rapid re-housing case management.

📞 1-800-SAL-ARMY 🌐 salarmychicago.org
Application Period 🔄 Rolling
Walk-in or call. Emergency services available same-day based on capacity.
Documents Needed Photo ID preferred but not required for emergency services. Income verification needed for longer-term programs.

Food Assistance

SNAP, food pantries, and nutrition programs in Cook County

SNAP (Food Stamps) via Illinois ABE Portal

Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program providing monthly benefits on an EBT card to purchase food at authorized retailers. Illinois uses expanded BBCE (Broad-Based Categorical Eligibility) with higher income limits than most states.

📞 1-800-843-6154 🌐 abe.illinois.gov
Application Period 🔄 Rolling
Apply anytime. Standard processing: 30 days. Expedited (emergency): 5 days if income is extremely low. Phone interview required within 10 days of applying.
Eligibility (FY 2026) Gross income ≤ 165% FPL (IL BBCE)
1 person: $1,696/mo · 2 people: $2,292/mo
4 people: $3,483/mo · 6 people: $4,675/mo
Seniors 60+ / disabled: up to 200% FPL. No asset test in IL.
Monthly Benefits (FY 2026) 1 person: up to $292/mo · 2 people: $536/mo
4 people: $975/mo · 6 people: $1,370/mo
Documents Needed
  • Photo ID (state ID, driver's license, work/school ID, or birth certificate)
  • Social Security numbers for all household members
  • Proof of income (pay stubs, SSA letter, unemployment)
  • Proof of housing costs (rent receipt, mortgage, utility bills)
How to Apply
🌐 ABE Online Portal (fastest) 📞 1-800-843-6154 🏢 Local IDHS Family Resource Center
SNAP

Greater Chicago Food Depository — Food Pantry Network

Cook County's primary food bank operating a network of 700+ food pantries, soup kitchens, and shelters. Serves 800,000+ people annually. Open to anyone — no application, proof of income, or ID required at most locations.

📞 (773) 247-3663 🌐 Find a Food Pantry Near You
Access ✅ Walk-In Available
Most pantries are open to anyone who walks in. No appointment or application needed. Call the pantry first to confirm hours.
Documents Needed None required at most locations. Some sites may ask for a name and zip code for reporting purposes only.
How to Find a Pantry Use the interactive map at chicagosfoodbank.org/find-food to find pantries near you. Filter by hours, distance, and eligibility. 700+ locations across Cook County.
Food Bank

WIC — Women, Infants & Children Nutrition Program

Supplemental nutrition for pregnant women, new mothers, infants, and children up to age 5. Provides food vouchers for milk, cereal, eggs, fruits/vegetables, and infant formula, plus nutrition education and healthcare referrals.

📞 1-800-504-1110 🌐 Illinois WIC
Eligibility 🔄 Rolling
Pregnant, breastfeeding, or postpartum women; infants and children ≤ 5. Income ≤ 185% FPL. Automatic eligibility if on Medicaid, SNAP, or TANF.
Documents Needed
  • ID for parent/guardian
  • Proof of income (pay stubs) or Medicaid/SNAP card
  • Proof of residency (utility bill, lease)
  • Proof of pregnancy or child's birth certificate
WIC

Faith-Based & Worship Community Programs

Churches, synagogues, mosques, and faith-based organizations serving Cook County

Catholic Charities of the Archdiocese of Chicago

One of Cook County's largest social service networks. Operates 55+ food pantries across Cook and Lake Counties, plus emergency financial assistance, immigration services, housing counseling, senior services, and veteran support. Open to all, regardless of faith.

📞 (312) 655-7700 🌐 catholiccharities.net
Services Offered
  • 55+ food pantries & community meal sites
  • Emergency financial assistance (rent, utilities)
  • Immigration & naturalization legal services
  • Counseling & mental health services
  • Senior in-home care & day programs
  • Veterans services & substance abuse programs
Access ✅ Open to All
No faith requirement. Most food pantries serve anyone who walks in. Financial assistance may require intake appointment. Call or visit website for specific location hours.

  • Emergency shelter & transitional housing
  • Utility & rent assistance

Employment & Unemployment

Unemployment benefits, job training, and workforce programs

IDES — Illinois Unemployment Insurance

State unemployment benefits for Cook County workers who lost their job through no fault of their own. Weekly payments while you search for new employment, with up to 26 weeks of benefits.

📞 1-800-244-5631 🌐 ides.illinois.gov
Application Period 🔄 File Anytime
File immediately after losing your job. Claims are retroactive to the week you file. Online portal available 24/7 (except 8–10 PM daily).
Eligibility Must have earned at least $1,600 in base period with $440+ outside your highest-earning quarter. Separated from employer through no fault of your own. Must be able and available to work.
Weekly Benefits (2026) Individual: $51–$628/week
With non-working spouse: up to $748/week
With dependent children: up to $859/week
Up to 26 weeks of benefits.
How to File
🌐 IDES Online (24/7) 📞 1-800-244-5631 (M–F 8:30–5) 🏢 IDES Office (by appt)
