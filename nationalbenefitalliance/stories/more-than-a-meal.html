More Than a Meal: How a Food Pantry Helped a Single Mom Build a New Future

Source Westside Food Bank
Published 2026
Category Food Security

Maya worked as a maid, cleaning other people's homes for minimum wage while raising two daughters alone. Every month, she faced an impossible choice: pay the bills or feed her daughters. More often than not, she chose to skip meals so that her girls could eat. It was a pattern she had resigned herself to, until the day someone told her about the food pantry.

The Westside Food Bank changed everything. When Maya first walked through the door, she found more than groceries. She found people who understood her struggle. The pantry staff didn't just hand her bags of food; they introduced her to case managers who asked the bigger questions: "What do you want for your future? How can we help you get there?"

Weekly trips to the food pantry meant Maya could stop skipping meals. With that burden lifted, she was able to think beyond survival. With case managers at her side, Maya enrolled in a GED program. She studied while her daughters slept, determined to break the cycle of poverty that had trapped her. Meanwhile, the case managers helped her navigate childcare programs, ensuring her youngest could attend preschool while she was in class.

Today, Maya holds her GED diploma with pride. More importantly, she's mapped out her path forward. She's applying to community college with a scholarship grant that case managers helped her secure. She dreams of becoming a social worker, helping other families navigate the programs that saved her life. "The food pantry gave me groceries," Maya says, "but they gave me something more important—they gave me hope that my life could be different."

Organizations like Westside Food Bank understand that food assistance is about more than daily meals. When families have their basic needs met, they can pursue education, build skills, and create stable futures. That holistic approach—combining food assistance with case management, childcare support, and educational opportunities—transforms lives and breaks generational cycles of poverty.
