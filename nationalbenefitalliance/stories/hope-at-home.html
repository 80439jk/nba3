Hope at Home: A Small Minnesota Fund Distributing $60,000 to Keep Families Housed

Source Post Bulletin
Published 2026
Location Rochester, Minnesota

In Rochester, Minnesota, a small organization called Family Promise Rochester had an ambitious idea: create a fund to prevent evictions before they happen. The result, the Hope at Home Fund, represents something powerful—a community deciding that housing is too important to leave to chance, that families deserve a safety net when unexpected hardship strikes.

The Hope at Home Fund was created specifically to provide emergency rental assistance to families facing eviction. With $60,000 in initial funding, the program has already begun changing lives. When a family receives an eviction notice or falls behind on rent due to job loss, medical emergency, or other crisis, they can apply for emergency assistance to stay in their home.

Impact by the Numbers: Based on national data, a $60,000 emergency fund can help 15-25 families avoid eviction. Each prevented eviction saves not just a family's home, but also reduces the ripple effects: children stay in school, adults maintain employment, health improves, and families remain connected to their communities.

Family Promise Rochester's approach combines emergency financial assistance with case management. Staff work with families to address the root causes of housing instability—connecting people with job training, mental health services, substance abuse treatment, and ongoing support. The goal is not just to prevent one eviction, but to build stability for the long term.

The Hope at Home Fund is part of a larger movement across America recognizing that emergency rental assistance is one of the most cost-effective investments in human welfare. Studies show that preventing eviction costs far less than addressing homelessness afterward. Eviction prevention keeps families in their homes, children in school, and people engaged with their communities and employment.

What makes the Hope at Home Fund special is that it's locally grown, community-supported, and deeply personal. When people in Rochester learned about the need, they responded. Donors, foundations, and local leaders came together to say: "In our community, families don't lose their homes to circumstance." That spirit of collective responsibility is replicating across the country, creating a patchwork of safety nets—large government programs and small local funds working together to ensure that housing remains a foundation for dignity and opportunity.
