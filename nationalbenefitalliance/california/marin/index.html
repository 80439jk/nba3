Utility Assistance

Struggling with electricity, gas, or water bills? Marin County residents may qualify for LIHEAP energy assistance and the REACH Program to reduce monthly utility costs.

Marin County Health & Human Services

Phone: 415-473-3000

Website: https://www.marinhhs.org

Apply for bill payment assistance, weatherization, and energy crisis programs. Income-eligible households may receive up to $1,000 in annual utility support.

Programs Available:

  • LIHEAP – Federal heating and cooling bill assistance
  • REACH Program – Emergency utility shutoff prevention
  • California Alternate Rates for Energy (CARE) – Up to 30% discount on utility bills
  • Energy Savings Assistance (ESA) – Free home weatherization
🏠

Housing & Rental Assistance

Facing eviction or struggling to pay rent? Marin County offers several programs to help residents stay housed and find affordable housing options.

Marin Housing Authority

Phone: 415-491-2525

Website: https://www.marinhousing.org

Housing vouchers (Section 8), emergency rental assistance, and eviction prevention resources for qualifying low-income residents of Marin County.

Programs Available:

  • Section 8 / HCV – Housing Choice Voucher rental subsidy
  • Emergency Rental Assistance – COVID-19 and ongoing relief funds
  • Homeless Prevention – Deposit and first/last month rent help
  • CalWORKs Housing Support – Housing stability for families
🍎

Food Assistance

No one in Marin County should go hungry. Access free food through CalFresh, WIC, and local food banks throughout the county.

Marin Food Bank

Phone: 415-883-1302

Website: https://www.marinfoodbank.org

Free food distribution, mobile pantry events, and referrals to CalFresh for Marin County residents experiencing food insecurity.

Programs Available:

  • CalFresh (SNAP) – Monthly grocery benefits EBT card
  • WIC – Nutrition support for women, infants, and children
  • School Meals – Free and reduced-price breakfast and lunch
  • Summer Food Program – Free meals for children in summer
🏥

Healthcare & Medi-Cal

California's Medi-Cal program provides free or low-cost health coverage to millions of residents. Marin County residents can apply through the county or online.

Covered California & BenefitsCal

Phone: 800-300-1506

Website: www.coveredca.com

Medi-Cal, Covered California plans, and Covered CA with Advanced Premium Tax Credits for individuals and families in Marin County.

Programs Available:

  • Medi-Cal – Free health coverage for low-income adults and families
  • Children's Health Initiative – Coverage for all kids regardless of status
  • Family PACT – Free family planning services
  • Local Community Health Centers – Sliding scale care in San Rafael
💼

Employment & Unemployment

Looking for work or recently laid off? California EDD and local workforce programs can help Marin County residents with unemployment benefits and job placement.

California EDD & Marin County Workforce Development

Phone: 800-300-5616 (EDD)

Website: www.edd.ca.gov

File for UI benefits, find job training, resume help, and career centers serving Marin County county residents.

Programs Available:

  • Unemployment Insurance (UI) – Weekly benefits up to $450/week
  • Paid Family Leave (PFL) – Up to 8 weeks for family care
  • State Disability Insurance (SDI) – Short-term disability payments
  • CalJOBS – Job listings and career services statewide
👴

Senior Services

Older adults in Marin County have access to a variety of programs including meal delivery, transportation, in-home care, and Medicare assistance.

Marin County Area Agency on Aging

Phone: 211

Website: https://www.marincounty.org

Senior nutrition, transportation, health insurance counseling (HICAP), and in-home supportive services (IHSS) for Marin County seniors.

Programs Available:

  • Meals on Wheels – Home meal delivery for homebound seniors
  • IHSS – In-Home Supportive Services for daily living
  • HICAP – Free Medicare counseling and benefits review
  • Senior Transportation – Rides to medical appointments
♿

Disability Services

Californians with disabilities in Marin County can access SSI, SSDI, Regional Center services, and ADA accommodations through county and state programs.

California Department of Rehabilitation & SSA

Phone: 800-952-5544 (DOR)

Website: www.dor.ca.gov

Vocational rehabilitation, assistive technology, disability income support, and independent living services for Marin County residents with disabilities.

Programs Available:

  • SSI/SSDI – Federal disability income benefits
  • Regional Center – Services for developmental disabilities
  • Vocational Rehabilitation – Job training and placement
  • Assistive Technology – Devices and accommodations support
👶

Child Care & Education

Working families in Marin County may qualify for subsidized child care, Head Start, and education assistance to reduce out-of-pocket costs.

California Department of Social Services – Child Care

Phone: 916-651-8848

Website: cdss.ca.gov

CalWORKs child care, Alternative Payment Program, Head Start, and state preschool slots for income-eligible families in Marin County.

Programs Available:

  • CalWORKs Child Care – Subsidized care for working families
  • Head Start / Early Head Start – Free preschool for children 0–5
  • California State Preschool – Part and full-day preschool
  • Child Care Resource & Referral – Local provider search and subsidies
🤝

Community & Crisis Services

For immediate help or ongoing support, Marin County's community action agencies and crisis lines provide rapid-response assistance 24/7.

Community Action Marin

Phone: 415-526-7580

Website: https://www.communityactionmarin.org

Emergency assistance, case management, and community support programs serving low-income residents throughout Marin County.

211 Marin County

Phone: 211

Website: https://www.211marin.org

Free, confidential referrals to local services 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. Available in multiple languages.
