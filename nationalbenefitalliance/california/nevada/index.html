Utility Assistance

Struggling with electricity, gas, or water bills? Nevada County residents may qualify for LIHEAP energy assistance and the REACH Program to reduce monthly utility costs.

Nevada County Social Services

Phone: 530-265-1639

Website: https://www.mynevadacounty.com/socialservices

Apply for bill payment assistance, weatherization, and energy crisis programs. Income-eligible households may receive up to $1,000 in annual utility support.

Programs Available:

  • LIHEAP – Federal heating and cooling bill assistance
  • REACH Program – Emergency utility shutoff prevention
  • California Alternate Rates for Energy (CARE) – Up to 30% discount on utility bills
  • Energy Savings Assistance (ESA) – Free home weatherization
🏠

Housing & Rental Assistance

Facing eviction or struggling to pay rent? Nevada County offers several programs to help residents stay housed and find affordable housing options.

Nevada County Housing Authority

Phone: 530-265-1639

Website: https://www.mynevadacounty.com

Housing vouchers (Section 8), emergency rental assistance, and eviction prevention resources for qualifying low-income residents of Nevada County.

Programs Available:

  • Section 8 / HCV – Housing Choice Voucher rental subsidy
  • Emergency Rental Assistance – COVID-19 and ongoing relief funds
  • Homeless Prevention – Deposit and first/last month rent help
  • CalWORKs Housing Support – Housing stability for families
🍎

Food Assistance

No one in Nevada County should go hungry. Access free food through CalFresh, WIC, and local food banks throughout the county.

Interfaith Food Ministry Nevada County

Phone: 530-272-0425

Website: https://www.ifmfoodbank.org

Free food distribution, mobile pantry events, and referrals to CalFresh for Nevada County residents experiencing food insecurity.

Programs Available:

  • CalFresh (SNAP) – Monthly grocery benefits EBT card
  • WIC – Nutrition support for women, infants, and children
  • School Meals – Free and reduced-price breakfast and lunch
  • Summer Food Program – Free meals for children in summer
🏥

Healthcare & Medi-Cal

California's Medi-Cal program provides free or low-cost health coverage to millions of residents. Nevada County residents can apply through the county or online.

Covered California & BenefitsCal

Phone: 800-300-1506

Website: www.coveredca.com

Medi-Cal, Covered California plans, and Covered CA with Advanced Premium Tax Credits for individuals and families in Nevada County.

Programs Available:

  • Medi-Cal – Free health coverage for low-income adults and families
  • Children's Health Initiative – Coverage for all kids regardless of status
  • Family PACT – Free family planning services
  • Local Community Health Centers – Sliding scale care in Nevada City
💼

Employment & Unemployment

Looking for work or recently laid off? California EDD and local workforce programs can help Nevada County residents with unemployment benefits and job placement.

California EDD & Nevada County Workforce Development

Phone: 800-300-5616 (EDD)

Website: www.edd.ca.gov

File for UI benefits, find job training, resume help, and career centers serving Nevada County county residents.

Programs Available:

  • Unemployment Insurance (UI) – Weekly benefits up to $450/week
  • Paid Family Leave (PFL) – Up to 8 weeks for family care
  • State Disability Insurance (SDI) – Short-term disability payments
  • CalJOBS – Job listings and career services statewide
👴

Senior Services

Older adults in Nevada County have access to a variety of programs including meal delivery, transportation, in-home care, and Medicare assistance.

Nevada County Area Agency on Aging

Phone: 530-265-1639

Website: https://www.mynevadacounty.com

Senior nutrition, transportation, health insurance counseling (HICAP), and in-home supportive services (IHSS) for Nevada County seniors.

Programs Available:

  • Meals on Wheels – Home meal delivery for homebound seniors
  • IHSS – In-Home Supportive Services for daily living
  • HICAP – Free Medicare counseling and benefits review
  • Senior Transportation – Rides to medical appointments
♿

Disability Services

Californians with disabilities in Nevada County can access SSI, SSDI, Regional Center services, and ADA accommodations through county and state programs.

California Department of Rehabilitation & SSA

Phone: 800-952-5544 (DOR)

Website: www.dor.ca.gov

Vocational rehabilitation, assistive technology, disability income support, and independent living services for Nevada County residents with disabilities.

Programs Available:

  • SSI/SSDI – Federal disability income benefits
  • Regional Center – Services for developmental disabilities
  • Vocational Rehabilitation – Job training and placement
  • Assistive Technology – Devices and accommodations support
👶

Child Care & Education

Working families in Nevada County may qualify for subsidized child care, Head Start, and education assistance to reduce out-of-pocket costs.

California Department of Social Services – Child Care

Phone: 916-651-8848

Website: cdss.ca.gov

CalWORKs child care, Alternative Payment Program, Head Start, and state preschool slots for income-eligible families in Nevada County.

Programs Available:

  • CalWORKs Child Care – Subsidized care for working families
  • Head Start / Early Head Start – Free preschool for children 0–5
  • California State Preschool – Part and full-day preschool
  • Child Care Resource & Referral – Local provider search and subsidies
🤝

Emergency assistance, case management, and community support programs serving low-income residents throughout Nevada County.
